# BrowserSync — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-21_

BrowserSync syncs your bookmarks, open tabs, and browsing history between your
own browsers. **Your data goes only where you point it.**

## What data is processed
- Bookmarks, open-tab URLs/titles, and history visits (URL, title, time) — only
  for the data types you enable.
- Configuration you enter (transport URL/credentials, passphrase, device name,
  filters).

## Where it goes
- BrowserSync has **no servers** and the authors receive **none** of your data.
- Your data is written only to the **destination you configure**: a WebDAV
  server, a self-hosted server, a local file via the companion agent, or your
  browser's own storage. You control that destination.
- If you point a transport at a third party (e.g. a hosted WebDAV/cloud folder),
  that provider's privacy policy applies to the data stored there. Enable
  **end-to-end encryption** to ensure they only ever see ciphertext.

## Stored locally
- Configuration, sync bookkeeping, and bookmark backups are kept in the
  browser's local extension storage on your device. Uninstalling the extension
  removes them.

## No tracking
- No analytics, no telemetry, no advertising, no third-party trackers. The only
  network requests are to the sync destination you configure.
